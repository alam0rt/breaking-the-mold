What are all the enemies in the game
The enemies in Skullmonkeys range from standard foot soldiers to environmental hazards and large-scale bosses. Most common enemies can be defeated by Klaymen's "butt-bounce", though several require specific weapons or tactics due to armour or projectiles
.
Standard and Specialist Monkeys
• Clay Keeper: These monkeys sit still or lumber back and forth while snacking on clay; hitting them provides a free clay ball
.
• Loud Mouth: Monkeys that run around flailing their arms with a long reach
.
• Mental Monkey: A faster version of the Loud Mouth that moves at double speed when flailing
.
• Robot Hover Monkey: In early levels, these are indestructible and drop deadly laser balls from above
.
• Tempest Pulsating Monkey: These glow periodically; if you butt-bounce them while they are glowing, you will be "fried"
.
• Head Shooter Monkey: These monkeys launch their own heads, which float towards Klaymen to bite him
.
• Jumpy the Gorilla: A large monkey that jumps constantly; you can run under him or bounce on him
.
• Triple Laser Butt Bounce Monkey: When defeated, these leave behind a dangerous cluster of glowing laser balls
.
• Egg-Beater: These have razor-sharp propellers on their heads, making them immune to butt-bouncing
.
• JX1137 Test Pilot: A monkey equipped with a rocketpack
.
• El Barfo: These gruesome enemies barf themselves out of their own skin
.
• Super Bomber Monk: Pilots that guard the "Drivey Runn" levels by dropping bombs
.
• Flapper: Monkeys that fly using hand fans; they regenerate quickly after being hit
.

.

What are all the items
Items in Skullmonkeys range from standard collectibles to powerful screen-clearing weapons and utility objects that alter Klaymen's physical state.
Core Collectibles and Lives
• Orange Balls (Clay): These floating orbs are scattered throughout the levels; gathering 100 of them grants Klaymen an extra life
.
• Klaymen's Head (1up): These items resemble the hero's head and provide an instant extra life
.
• Swirly Qs (Swirls): If you collect three of these in a single world, you gain access to a bonus level at the end of the stage
. You must specifically jump into the teleport ball marked with a swirl to enter
.
• 1970s Icons: There are only three of these hidden in the entire game
. Collecting all three unlocks a secret 1970s-themed bonus room filled with lava lamps and disco-era relics
.
Defensive and Utility Items
• Halo: This glowing ring acts as a shield, absorbing one hit from an enemy or environmental hazard
. If Klaymen collects a second halo while already wearing one, he receives clay instead
.
• Hamsters (Hamster Shield): Three furry hamsters circle Klaymen, protecting him by killing the first three enemies they touch before they disappear
.
• Glidey Bird (Yellow Bird): This friend follows Klaymen and allows him to glide across long distances when the jump button (X) is held down
.
• Ma Bird: This stationary bird functions as a checkpoint; if Klaymen dies, he will restart the level from the bird's location
.
• Super Willie (The Head): Activated with R2, Klaymen’s cousin Willie Trombone pops out of his chest and spins around to automatically gather every power-up currently visible on the screen
. Klaymen can carry up to seven of these
.
Offensive Weapons
• Green Bullets (Energy Balls): These serve as standard ammo for Klaymen's projectile attack, fired by pressing Circle
. While the game manual states Klaymen can carry 20 bullets, some guides suggest a limit of nine
.
• Phoenix Hand (The Bird): Pressing L1 releases a bird that seeks out and destroys the nearest on-screen enemy
. Klaymen can carry a maximum of seven
.
• Universe Enema (Super Power): This pink energy ball is Klaymen’s most powerful weapon; pressing R1 destroys every enemy currently on the screen
. Klaymen can hold seven at a time
.
• Phart Head (Fart Clone): Activated with L2, Klaymen creates a gaseous "klone" of himself to scout ahead
. If Klaymen is overwhelmed while the clone is active, he receives a second chance starting from where the clone was first released
.
Environmental and Status Items
• Green Hearts: These shrink Klaymen on contact, which is necessary for crawling through small gaps and jumping on specific enemies that are otherwise dangerous
.
• Yellow Chevrons: These are used to restore Klaymen to his normal height after being shrunk by a Green Heart
.
• Clocks: These serve as temporary platforms that explode two seconds after being jumped on, often hinting at nearby secrets
.
• Treasure Balls: These spotted yellow balls float in the air and must be butt-bounced to reveal the hidden items inside
.
• Teleport Balls: Found at the end of levels, these allow Klaymen to progress to the next area
. Tan or orange balls are standard, while red balls lead to more difficult versions of the subsequent level
.
• Virtual Platforms (Activators): These appear as spotlight-like objects that Klaymen must jump on to activate temporary virtual platforms
.
